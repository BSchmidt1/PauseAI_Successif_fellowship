Completed · example project

152 German professors asking their government to back a global AI-safety agreement.

  • National press coverage
  • Passive influence on policymakers
  • Actively enabling an outreach round — 30+ meetings with federal policymakers
  • Potential starting point for a coalition of scientists in Germany
01

Red lines

AI carries the risk of unacceptable global consequences. Clear limits are needed that must not be crossed.

02

Binding safety standards

The uncontrolled AI race leads to the neglect of safety. Regulation with independent international enforcement is required.

Signatories include Fields Medallist Peter Scholze (MPI for Mathematics, Bonn) and Max Planck Medal recipient Andrzej Buras (TU Munich), alongside researchers in computer science, physics, philosophy, psychology, medicine and many other disciplines at universities across Germany.

This was only a test: we contacted roughly 5,000 of Germany's ~50,000 professors.

Now far cheaper The playbook, templates and pipeline to produce the contacts exist — a fellow could reproduce this for a fraction of the hours, an estimated 20–60h.

Results

  • 5 university talks planned.
  • 3 politicians who had ignored our emails got in touch — and said they had passed the information on.
  • A local MP accepted a meeting she had previously ignored (20 professors at her city's university had signed).
  • A follow-up cold-outreach campaign with 30+ parliamentarian meetings scheduled.
